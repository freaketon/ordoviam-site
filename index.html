01 · The road today

Mid-market debt is a vast landscape with no map.

The lenders a company needs exist — thousands of them. But the path between a CFO and the right desk is unmarked: no real-time picture of lender appetite, a different package for every desk, and rejections that arrive without reasons. Raising debt happens once every few years; the landscape shifts every quarter.

05 · Questions

Plain answers.

What is capital wayfinding?

A structured, documented approach to the mid-market debt search. Instead of months of cold outreach across a fragmented lender landscape, the search becomes a sequence of recorded stations — intake, package, match, outreach, terms — so you always know who was approached, on what basis, and with what response.

Who is Ordoviam for?

Companies in the lower-middle market — roughly $10M–$150M in revenue raising $5M–$50M in debt — and the CFOs, sponsors, and finance teams running those raises. On the supply side: lender desks that want pre-qualified, on-mandate deal flow.

Do you recommend lenders or guarantee financing?

No. Ordoviam structures and documents the search. Mandate fit is established from the borrower’s deal profile before any desk sees a name, and fit is never for sale. We don’t recommend lenders, rank them for payment, or guarantee outcomes — protected passage, not guaranteed arrival.

What does “mandate-fit-first” mean?

Every desk has a mandate: size range, sector, geography, structure. Mandate-fit-first means those questions are answered before outreach happens — desks only see deals in their box, and borrowers don’t burn weeks on dead-end conversations.

What is “the record you can defend”?

A documented trail of the entire search: which desks were approached, what was sent, on what basis, and what came back. It’s the artifact a CFO can put in front of a board or sponsor to show the process was disciplined and complete.

Why “the wayfinder”?

From ordo (an order with a duty) and via (the way): a structured body whose job is to make a difficult journey passable. We mark the route through a fragmented capital landscape. We don’t promise the destination.
