04 · Questions

Plain answers.

What is capital wayfinding?

A structured, documented approach to the mid-market debt search. Instead of months of cold outreach across a fragmented lender landscape, the search becomes a sequence of recorded stations (intake, package, match, outreach, terms), so you always know who was approached, on what basis, and with what response.

Who is Ordoviam for?

Companies in the lower-middle market, roughly $10M to $150M in revenue raising $5M to $50M in debt, and the CFOs, sponsors, and finance teams running those raises. On the supply side: lender desks that want pre-qualified, on-mandate deal flow.

Do you recommend lenders or guarantee financing?

No. Ordoviam structures and documents the search. Mandate fit is established from the borrower’s deal profile before any desk sees a name, and fit is never for sale. We don’t recommend lenders, rank them for payment, or guarantee outcomes. Protected passage, not guaranteed arrival.

What does “mandate-fit-first” mean?

Every desk has a mandate: size range, sector, geography, structure. Mandate-fit-first means those questions are answered before outreach happens. Desks only see deals in their box, and borrowers don’t burn weeks on dead-end conversations.

What is “the record you can defend”?

A documented trail of the entire search: which desks were approached, what was sent, on what basis, and what came back: the artifact a CFO can put in front of a board or sponsor.

Why “the wayfinder”?

From ordo (an order with a duty) and via (the way): a structured body whose job is to make a difficult journey passable. We mark the route. We don’t promise the destination.
